The current trends

Globally, teaching is one of the largest professions, with millions of educators working across various educational systems. According to UNESCO, over 69 million teachers need to be recruited by 2030 to meet global education goals. This includes 24.4 million primary school teachers and 44.4 million secondary school teachers(reference)

What about the drawbacks?

Teachers often feel underpaid for the amount of work they do, with many seeking significant pay raises. This dissatisfaction is widespread, with educators reporting salary expectations much higher than their current earnings.

Teachers face long hours and high stress, working well beyond the typical 40-hour workweek. Many report burnout due to excessive workloads and classroom management challenges(reference)

And what about the benefits?

Teaching offers the opportunity to make a significant impact on students' lives and shape future generations. Many teachers report high levels of job satisfaction, particularly when they witness student growth and success(reference)
